MetaMask / EOA Wallet Setup

You connected Polymarket with MetaMask or another web3 wallet (Coinbase Wallet, Rainbow, etc.). Your wallet is an Externally Owned Account (EOA) — you hold the private key directly. The bot uses that same key to sign and submit trades.

Recommended: use a dedicated bot wallet

For security, it's strongly recommended to create a separate wallet specifically for the bot rather than using your primary Polymarket wallet. Transfer only the USDC you want the bot to trade with into this dedicated wallet.

Using your main wallet — if the key is ever leaked, all assets are at risk
Dedicated bot wallet — risk is limited to the bot's trading balance only

Step-by-step setup

1

Create a dedicated bot wallet in MetaMask

Skip this step if you are happy to use your existing Polymarket wallet directly.

  1. a.Open MetaMask → click the account selector at the top → Add account or hardware wallet → Create a new account.
  2. b.Name it "PMBot" or similar so it's clearly identifiable.
  3. c.Note its wallet address — you'll connect this account to Polymarket next.
2

Connect the wallet to Polymarket & deposit USDC

  1. a.Go to polymarket.com and click Connect wallet. Select MetaMask and approve the connection with your bot wallet account.
  2. b.Click Deposit and follow the on-screen steps to deposit USDC to your Polymarket account on Polygon. You can bridge from Ethereum or send USDC directly on the Polygon network.
  3. c.Place any small trade on Polymarket — this triggers the contract approval setup automatically (USDC allowance + CTF token approval). The bot will also verify/set these on startup.

For sig_type=0, the bot automatically checks and sets USDC and CTF contract approvals on every startup — no manual step needed after the initial Polymarket connection.

3

Export your private key from MetaMask

Only export the key for the dedicated bot wallet.

Do not paste your key in any browser window, chat, or document. Only enter it in the bot's settings page or .env file on your server.

  1. a.In MetaMask, make sure you are on the bot wallet account.
  2. b.Click the three-dot menu (⋮) → Account details.
  3. c.Click Show private key, enter your MetaMask password to confirm.
  4. d.Copy the 64-character hex string. It may start with 0x — both formats are accepted by the bot.

This will be your

PRIVATE_KEY=0xabc123...def456
4

Enter your credentials in the bot dashboard

In the bot's web dashboard, navigate to Settings and fill in:

Field Value Notes
PRIVATE_KEY Your bot wallet private key 64-char hex, optional 0x prefix
FUNDER_ADDRESS Leave blank Defaults to the address derived from your private key
SIGNATURE_TYPE 0 Standard EOA — this is the default
DRY_RUN true → false Start in dry-run to verify, then disable

On first startup, the bot will automatically run a $1 connection test — it buys and immediately sells 1 share to verify everything is working. This only happens once per wallet.

Checklist

  • □ Created a dedicated bot wallet in MetaMask (recommended)
  • □ Connected that wallet to Polymarket and deposited USDC
  • □ Made one small trade on Polymarket to register contract approvals
  • □ Exported the bot wallet's private key
  • □ Entered PRIVATE_KEY and SIGNATURE_TYPE=0 in the dashboard
  • □ Verified connection in dry-run mode before going live

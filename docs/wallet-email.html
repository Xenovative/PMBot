Email / Magic Account Setup

You signed up to Polymarket with an email address (or Google/Apple). Your funds are held in a Magic-managed custodial wallet. The bot cannot directly access that wallet — instead, you create a separate EOA "proxy signer" wallet and delegate trading permissions to it.

How this works

1

Your Funder Wallet

Polymarket custodial wallet that holds your USDC. You never export this key.

delegates to

2

Proxy Signer Wallet

A new EOA wallet you create. The bot uses its private key to sign orders on behalf of the funder.

Step-by-step setup

1

Find your Polymarket funder wallet address

This is the wallet address Polymarket created for you. You need it as your FUNDER_ADDRESS.

  1. a.Go to polymarket.com and log in with your email.
  2. b.Click your profile icon (top right) → Profile.
  3. c.Your wallet address appears below your username — it looks like 0xABCD...1234. Copy it.
  4. d.Alternatively, go to Settings → Wallet — the address is shown there too.

This will be your

FUNDER_ADDRESS=0xYourPolymarketWalletAddress
2

Create a new proxy signer wallet (MetaMask)

You need a brand-new, dedicated EOA wallet to act as the proxy signer. Do not reuse a wallet that holds significant funds.

  1. a.Install MetaMask (browser extension) if you don't have it.
  2. b.Open MetaMask → click the account selector at the top → Add account or hardware wallet → Create a new account.
  3. c.Give it a name like "PMBot Signer". Note its wallet address — this is the proxy signer address.
  4. d.You do not need to fund this wallet with USDC — funds come from your Polymarket funder wallet.
3

Export the proxy signer private key

Only export the key for the dedicated proxy signer wallet you just created — never your main wallet or your Polymarket funder wallet.

  1. a.In MetaMask, switch to the proxy signer account.
  2. b.Click the three-dot menu (⋮) → Account details.
  3. c.Click Show private key, enter your MetaMask password, and reveal the key.
  4. d.Copy the 64-character hex string (it may or may not start with 0x — both are fine).

This will be your

PRIVATE_KEY=0xabc123...def456
4

Register the proxy signer on Polymarket

Polymarket must know that your proxy signer wallet is allowed to trade on behalf of your funder wallet. This is done via one transaction in the Polymarket UI.

  1. a.While logged in to Polymarket with your email account, go to any open market.
  2. b.Place a small trade (e.g. buy $1 of any outcome). This triggers contract approval setup automatically.
  3. c.Polymarket will prompt you to link/approve your wallet — follow the on-screen steps.

The bot (sig_type=1) skips automatic approval — this manual step is required once for your account to function.

5

Enter your credentials in the bot dashboard

In the bot's web dashboard, navigate to Settings and fill in:

Field Value Notes
PRIVATE_KEY Your proxy signer key 64-char hex, optional 0x prefix
FUNDER_ADDRESS Your Polymarket email wallet address 0x... 42-char address
SIGNATURE_TYPE 1 Required for Magic/email accounts
DRY_RUN true → false Start in dry-run to verify, then disable

Checklist

  • □ Copied your Polymarket funder wallet address
  • □ Created a new dedicated proxy signer wallet in MetaMask
  • □ Exported the proxy signer's private key
  • □ Completed one manual trade on Polymarket to register contract approvals
  • □ Entered PRIVATE_KEY, FUNDER_ADDRESS, and SIGNATURE_TYPE=1 in the dashboard
  • □ Verified connection in dry-run mode before going live

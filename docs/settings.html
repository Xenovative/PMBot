Bot Settings Reference

Every parameter available in the bot's web dashboard Settings panel. Changes take effect immediately — no restart required — unless noted otherwise.

Where to change settings

Open your bot's web dashboard → click Settings (gear icon). All fields here map 1:1 to the environment variables listed below. Credentials are write-only — they are never displayed after saving.

Credentials

Required for live trading. Leave PRIVATE_KEY blank to run in dry-run simulation mode.

PRIVATE_KEY string

The private key used to sign all orders and on-chain transactions. Must be a 32-byte hex string (64 hex characters), with or without the 0x prefix.

EOA (sig_type=0)

The private key of your trading wallet — the same wallet you connected to Polymarket.

Email/Magic (sig_type=1)

The private key of your proxy signer wallet — a separate EOA you created, not your email password.

FUNDER_ADDRESS string

The wallet address that holds funds on Polymarket. Format: 0x... (42 characters).

EOA (sig_type=0)

Leave blank — the bot derives the address automatically from your private key.

Email/Magic (sig_type=1)

Required. Your Polymarket custodial wallet address — find it on your Polymarket profile page.

SIGNATURE_TYPE
default: 0 integer

Tells the bot how to authenticate with the Polymarket CLOB API.

0

EOA (default)

Standard MetaMask / web3 wallet. The private key is the trading wallet key. Bot handles on-chain approvals automatically.

1

Magic / email custodial

Email-based Polymarket account. Requires both PRIVATE_KEY (proxy signer) and FUNDER_ADDRESS. Contract approvals must be set manually via Polymarket UI.

2

Gnosis Safe

Smart contract multisig wallet. Requires both PRIVATE_KEY (proxy signer EOA) and FUNDER_ADDRESS (Safe address). Bot runs approvals via the proxy signer.

DRY_RUN
default: true boolean

When true, the bot scans markets and logs opportunities but never submits real orders. No funds are spent. Use this to verify your setup before going live.

⚠️ Set to false only after confirming the connection test passes in dry-run mode.

Trading Controls

Core parameters that control when and how much the bot trades.

Parameter Default Description
TARGET_PAIR_COST 0.99 Maximum total cost (USD) to trigger an arbitrage trade. When UP ask + DOWN ask ≤ this value, the bot enters. Must be < 1.00 to be profitable. Lower = fewer but higher-profit trades.
Example: 0.97 means only trade when the pair costs $0.97 or less, guaranteeing at least $0.03 profit per share.
ORDER_SIZE 50 Number of shares to buy per side in each arbitrage trade. Capped at 80% of available order book depth. Actual spend = ORDER_SIZE × price per side.
MAX_TRADES_PER_MARKET 10 Maximum number of arbitrage entries the bot will make on a single market within one market window. Prevents over-concentration in one position.
TRADE_COOLDOWN_SECONDS 60 Minimum wait time (seconds) between trades on the same market. Prevents the bot from entering the same market repeatedly when prices are volatile.
MAX_POSITION_IMBALANCE 5 Maximum allowed difference (shares) between your UP and DOWN holdings in a single market. Prevents one-sided exposure if an unwind fails.
MIN_LIQUIDITY 100 Minimum order book depth (shares) required on both sides before the bot will enter. Markets thinner than this are skipped to avoid slippage.

Markets & Symbols

Control which markets the bot monitors.

CRYPTO_SYMBOLS
default: btc,eth,sol string list

Comma-separated list of crypto symbols to monitor. The bot only scans UP/DOWN markets for these assets.

Examples

btc,eth,sol — Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana (default)
btc — Bitcoin only
btc,eth,sol,matic,link — Extended watchlist

Available symbols depend on what Polymarket currently has active UP/DOWN markets for. Adding a symbol with no active market simply results in it being skipped.

MIN_TIME_REMAINING_SECONDS
varies by bot integer

Markets expiring in fewer than this many seconds are skipped. Prevents entering a position just before resolution with no time to capture payout.

15m bot

120s

1h bot

600s

4h bot

600s

Daily bot

3600s

Bargain Hunter

Secondary strategy that buys one side of a market when it's very cheap, expecting the pair to eventually reach an arbitrage-able spread.

Bargain Hunter positions carry more risk than pure arbitrage — they are directional bets that one side recovers. Only enable it if you understand the risks and have sufficient balance to hold open positions.

Parameter Default Description
BARGAIN_ENABLED true Master switch. Set to false to disable Bargain Hunter entirely and run pure arbitrage only.
BARGAIN_PRICE_THRESHOLD 0.49 The bot buys a single side when its price drops below this threshold. E.g. 0.49 means buy when a side is trading at less than $0.49.
BARGAIN_MIN_PRICE 0.10 Minimum price to enter a Bargain Hunter position. Sides trading below this (e.g. <$0.10) are considered too risky and are skipped.
BARGAIN_PAIR_THRESHOLD 0.99 Once holding one side, watches for the pair sum to fall below this value — then completes the pair to lock in arbitrage profit. Should match or be slightly lower than TARGET_PAIR_COST.
BARGAIN_MAX_ROUNDS 56 Maximum scan rounds to hold a Bargain Hunter position before forcing a close. Prevents positions from being held indefinitely.
BARGAIN_STOP_LOSS_CENTS 0.02 Stop-loss threshold in USD per share. If the held side drops more than this from the entry price, the position is liquidated. Higher = more tolerance.
BARGAIN_STOP_LOSS_IMMUNE_ROUNDS 3 Number of initial scan rounds after entry where stop-loss is not checked. Prevents being stopped out immediately due to normal entry slippage.
BARGAIN_STOP_LOSS_DEFER_MINUTES 5–30 After a stop-loss is triggered, defers the actual exit by this many minutes. Avoids exiting on a brief spike down that recovers quickly.
BARGAIN_STOP_LOSS_COOLDOWN_MINUTES 5–30 After a stop-loss exit, waits this many minutes before entering a new Bargain Hunter position on the same market. Prevents re-entering a falling market immediately.
BARGAIN_FIRST_BUY_BIAS AUTO Controls which side the Bargain Hunter prefers to buy first.
AUTO — buys whichever side is cheaper
UP — always buys the UP side first
DOWN — always buys the DOWN side first
BARGAIN_PAIR_ESCALATION_MINUTES 15–180 After holding a single-side position for this many minutes, lowers the pair completion threshold to close sooner, even at smaller profit. Limits exposure time.

Advanced

Leave these at defaults unless you know what you're changing.

Parameter Default Description
LATE_LIQUIDATION_SECONDS 90 (15m and 1h bots only) When a market has fewer than this many seconds until expiry, the bot force-sells any held positions at market price rather than waiting for on-chain resolution. This recovers capital faster at a small cost.
POLYGON_RPC_URL polygon-rpc.com The Polygon RPC endpoint used for on-chain transactions (approvals, CTF merges). Replace with a private RPC (Alchemy, Infura, QuickNode) for better reliability under high load.
